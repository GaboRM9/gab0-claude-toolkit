### The Historical Parallel

```
## The Historical Parallel

This resembles Mexico in 1994–1995: massive foreign capital inflows, a
structurally overvalued currency sustained by high interest rates, a government
running optimistic fiscal assumptions, and a single external shock (then: the
Zapatista uprising + US rate hikes; now: Trump tariffs + cartel succession
violence) capable of triggering rapid capital flight.

The 1994 Tequila Crisis saw the peso devalue 50% in weeks and GDP contract
6.2% in 1995.

Where the analogy breaks down: Mexico's foreign reserves are far larger today,
USMCA provides a structural trade anchor that NAFTA didn't have in the same
way, and the nearshoring thesis is a multi-year secular trend with no 1994
equivalent. The conditions for a full crisis exist, but the shock absorbers are
also much stronger.
```

The historical parallel anchors probability estimates. Where it breaks down
is as important as where it holds.

## Notes

- **Research is silent.** The user never sees searches, fetches, or signal classification — only the final report.
- **Each run is unique.** Results reflect the live state of the web at invocation time.
- **Shorter horizons = sharper predictions.** 1-week forecasts will have higher confidence scores than 1-year forecasts.
- **Broad topics auto-narrow.** The skill picks the most signal-rich sub-topic and tells you at the top of the report.
- **Domain detection shapes research.** A cultural topic gets audience/backlash searches; a political topic gets polling/coalition searches. The skill adapts without prompting.
